SCOPE
The scope of the project consists of a marine terminal to receive 40,000 tonnes of 98% sulfuric acid per month in 10,000 to 20,000 DWT ships with an unloading rate of 500 tph and onshore facilities that include a booster pump station, storage tanks, truck loading stations, offices, living quarters, internal roads, parking areas and a perimeter fence (See Sketch 01 in Appendix 1).
The terminal will also be capable of receiving water and diesel from either sulfuric acid ships or separate vessels.
GENERAL INFORMA TION

DUTY AND SERVICE

The port facility will be designed to operate on an as required basis.

Acid shipments per month:	2-4 depending on ship size
Ship acid unloading time:	20 - 40 hours
Ship acid unloading rate:	500 tonnes per hour
Ship water unloading rate:	70 m3 per hour
Ship diesel unloading rate:	10 m3per hour
Working life:	20 years

The truck acid loading facility will be designed to operate 12 hours per day, 365 days per year.

Trucks loaded per day:	45
Truck loading time:	30 minutes
Truck loading rate:	60 tonnes per hour
Working life:	20 years

LOCAL CONDITIONS
Design minimum temperature	12°C
Design maximum temperature	30°C
Design rainfall	5 mm/hr

EQUIPMENT DESIGN PARAMETERS

Atmospheric Storage Tanks
For Acid Service:
	Design in general accordance with API 650 and NACE Standard RP0294-94, Standard Recommended Practice, Design, Fabrication, and Inspection of Tanks for the Storage of Concentrated Sulfuric Acid and Oleum at Ambient Temperatures.
Application of anodic protection is required to reduce the corrosion rate to 0.1 mm per year. Minimum tank corrosion allowance is 2.0 mm.
	Shell material will be selected based on API 650 requirements. The following material selection has proven satisfactory in the past:
Shell, bottom, roof: ASTM A-516 Gr. 70
Nozzle: ASTM A-106 Gr. B
Nozzle Flange: ASTM A-105
Shell Manhole Neck: ASTM A-516 Gr. 70
Reinforcing Pads: ASTM A-516 Gr. 70
	Manhole minimum 600mm diameter, one on the side of the tank, one on the roof. Side manhole to have neck thickness minimum 19 mm and be stress relieved prior to installation to reduce localized stresses and corrosion.
	Nozzles will project inside the vessel at least 300 mm (protection against nozzle erosion). Horizontal nozzles will be sloped slightly to prevent acid from dripping down tank wall.
	Fill line will be a 316 stainless steel dip leg that is located a minimum of 2.5 m from the tank wall, extends 2 m below the tank overflow, and has a 12 mm diameter siphon break.
Tank will include 4 spare nozzles, DN100 each, and a drain nozzle complete with valve and blind flange.
Tank roof will be accessible by ladders and platforms.
Roof vent required as per NACE Standard RP0294-94.
	Instrumentation includes suitable electronic level gauge. Submerged components shall be 316L SS material.
	Surface preparation of interior of the tank shall comply with SSPC-SP6 after field welding and testing is complete.
For Non-Acid Service:
Design in accordance with API 650.

Tank Spill Containment
For Acid Service:
	Spill containment system will include a single containment area to collect spills from whichever of six acid storage tanks.
Remote spill containment pond volume will be 110% of the largest tank.
	Spill containment pond will consist of reinforced concrete slab and walls, with adequate acid-resistant coating.
	Top of concrete for bases of equipment and structural steel will be above the maximum spilled liquid level.

End Suction Centrifugal Pumps
Pumps designed in accordance with ANSI B73.1.
	Pump will be end suction centrifugal complete with mechanical seal, coupling and coupling guard.
	Pump wetted parts will be from 316 stainless steel or Alloy 20 and suitable for sulfuric acid service.
Pump base will be from polymer concrete or other acid resistant material.
Pumps will include spray shields to protect operators from high pressure leaks.
Instrumentation includes pressure gauge on inlet and outlet.
	Provide control panel such that for pumps installed as pairs (one operating, one standby), each pump will alternate as primary.
Truck Weighbridge
Digital or analogue load cells.
	Above ground installation with steel or concrete deck, including side rail, 3.35 m wide, length to suit maximum truck length.
	Computer control to track shipments by truck identification number and include standard commercial information to track outgoing shipments.
Pipe
Pipe system will be designed in accordance with ASME B31.3 Process Piping
Pipe material for acid service shall be ASTM A106 Grade B seamless. Design fluid velocity shall be not higher than 1.5 m/s for carbon steel pipes. Joint gaskets shall be PTFE material.
	Pipe for sulfuric acid service will be fully welded, A106 Grade B seamless or SS 316L material. Flanged connections will be minimized.
	Pipe for diesel fuel service will be fully welded, A106 Grade B seamless material. Flanged connections will be minimized.
	Pipe material for potable water service shall be A53 Grade B carbon steel pipe, threaded connections.
Flange bolts and nuts shall be as per ASTM A193/B7 and ASTM 194/7.
	Where a flanged connection is required, it will be covered with a suitable acid proof cover including acid leak indicator (visible pH strip) to minimize danger of acid spray from loose flanged connections.
	Valves for acid service will be SS 316L body and internals. Automatic valves will have electric actuator, manual valves above 14” inch diameter will have gear operator.
Valves for diesel fuel service will be SS 316L body and internals.
Valves for potable water service will be brass body and Stainless Steel internal disc.

Safety Showers and Eyewash Stations
Safety showers and eyewash stations will conform to local codes and regulations.
	All areas where operators work during normal operation will be serviced by shower and eyewash stations. Showers will be accessed without obstruction and be within 15 meters of operator work stations (such as the truck loading rack or pump stations).
	Showers will be deluge type with a minimum capacity of 100 liters per minute for a minimum of 15 minutes. Water temperature will be ambient. Shower will have integrated water tank.
	Eyewash stations will deliver a minimum 1.5 liters per minute to both eyes for a minimum of 15 minutes.
Safety shower will include audio and visual alarms when operated, and will be indicated in
the control room.

All rotating equipment to be protected with OSHA approved guards.
	Where possible, electric motors, shafts, bearings and all other components requiring periodic maintenance shall be standardized to minimize spare parts and components inventory, where possible. These components will be available through manufacturers’ representatives based in Peru.

As a minimum, surface preparation of all steel (structural and mechanical) will be to SSPC SP6, and a two part, surface tolerant, self priming, high build epoxy paintwill be used in all exposed areas.

The facility will be a conventional berthing dock consisting of a lightweight trestle, a loading platform, berthing dolphins and mooring dolphins for unloading 10,000 to 20,000 DWT vessels. The trestle will support one sulfuric acid line, one potable water line and one diesel line.

Corrosion Protection
The following measures to protect the marine facilities against corrosion are included in the design:

DESIGN CRITERIA
The natural terrain at the area where the onshore facilities will be built has a fairly steep slope falling towards the SW. The onshore facilities will therefore be accommodated in terraces at different levels to optimize the required volumes of cut and fill.
Advantage will be taken of the different levels for drainage purposes.
If at all possible the truck loading stations will be placed sufficiently below the sulfuric acid storage tanks to allow loading by gravity. Alternatively there would need to be loading pumps

BUILDINGS

The perimeter fence shall be “Olympic" type with 2.4 m high chain link mesh plus 4 rows of barbed wire.
Reinforced concrete posts shall be used.
